Jamography

Jane Brocket is a woman of many talents. Rising to prominence in the domesticity scene back in 1907 2007 with the publication of "The Gentle Art of Domesticity" following her hugely successful Yarnstorm blog, Jane has been a visionary and influencer before influencers even became a thing.

In the summer of 2020, Jane discovered the delights of jam making. Always a humble lady, she began with some simple apricots sourced from her local marche in Le Havre, as one does. As lockdown began and she found herself at a classic Brocket loose end, she delved deeper into the depths of jams, exploring new fruits (hello, chuckleberries) and techniques (goodbye, bad marmalade).

As her ever faithful daughter/copycat/helper/number 1 fan, I found myself assisting in the labeling, ladeling, and application of lashings of jam to all edible surfaces. When I had to choose a project for this module, naturally a jamsite was the only option. And so, here we are.

Please explore the (somewhat limited) site, chuck a cheeky Instagram follow - link in footer - and who knows, maybe one day we will actually sell some of this stuff, but don't get your hopes up!
